# NeuroAI Mind Labs — Investor Pitch Deck Spec

**Date:** 2026-04-03
**Format:** 8-slide presentation
**Audience:** Foundation program officers
**Funding target:** $500K–$3M seed
**Framework:** Thiel contrarian principles adapted for nonprofit research

---

## Context

NeuroAI Mind Labs is an independent nonprofit research organization based in Gainesville, Florida. The founder has years of NSF-funded research in NeuroAI and emotion perception, active collaborations with University of Florida faculty, and peer-reviewed publications. The organization bridges brain science and AI to advance mental health through a closed-loop computational framework.

The pitch targets foundation program officers for seed funding to hire an initial research team and build computational infrastructure (Year 1), then launch a pilot study and develop a prototype tool (Year 2).

---

## Deck Structure: "The Hybrid" — Thiel Opening + Scientific Rigor

The deck opens with the organization's scientific identity, builds the case through the problem-solution-validation arc, and closes with urgency and invitation.

## Slide 2: "The Broken Paradigm"

**Function:** The problem no one is solving correctly

**Title:** The crisis is growing. The science isn't keeping up.

**Content — three points:**

1. **The mental health crisis is accelerating** — depression, anxiety, addiction, and neurodegeneration are among the defining challenges of our time
2. **Current science is fragmented** — research is siloed across cellular, circuit, system, and behavioral levels with no unifying framework connecting mechanism to function
3. **AI doesn't help yet** — modern AI can model behavior, but it cannot explain how emotion emerges from biological systems or how humans truly think and feel

**Bottom line (bold):** "We lack the ability to track, predict, and regulate emotional states — and current treatments fail to account for individual neural and physiological differences."

**Visual:** Three columns or icons representing the three failure points. Optional: a diagram showing disconnected levels of analysis (cells | circuits | systems | behavior) with gaps between them.

**Speaker notes (~90 sec):** Walk through each point. Close with: "This isn't a resource problem — it's a framework problem. And that's what we solve."

---

## Slide 3: "The Breakthrough — Our Approach"

**Function:** The closed-loop framework

**Title:** A closed-loop framework from neurons to mind

**Content:**

Core idea: Integrate biology, psychology, neuroscience, and AI into a single closed-loop framework that studies how emotion emerges and evolves across scales.

Four-layer model:

| Layer | What we do |
|---|---|
| **Biological Mechanisms** | Investigate cellular basis of emotion, neural populations, brain circuits, brain-body coupling |
| **Data Integration** | Integrate neural, physiological, and behavioral data into unified datasets |
| **Computational Modeling** | Formalize mechanisms into models that infer latent emotional states, predict their evolution, simulate outcomes |
| **Actionable Intelligence** | Closed-loop systems that test hypotheses, guide interventions in real-time, and adapt dynamically |

**Key differentiator (bold):** "Our models are not only descriptive — they are actionable. They enable real-time intervention guided by biological mechanism, not surface-level behavior."

**Visual:** Circular/loop diagram showing the four layers feeding into each other. Arrows flowing from biology up through modeling and back down into intervention, emphasizing the closed loop.

**Speaker notes (~2 min):** Walk through each layer briefly, then emphasize: "What makes this different is the loop. We don't just observe — we model, predict, intervene, and learn. That's what translation looks like."

## Slide 6: "The Roadmap"

**Function:** 24-month plan with clear milestones

**Title:** From foundation to translation in 24 months

**Content — two phases:**

**Year 1: Build the Engine**
- Recruit core research team (computational neuroscientists, AI/ML researchers)
- Build computational infrastructure and data pipelines
- Establish formal research partnerships and data-sharing agreements
- Publish foundational work establishing the closed-loop framework

**Year 2: Prove the Model**
- Launch pilot study applying framework to a specific condition (e.g., depression, anxiety)
- Develop prototype tool for monitoring/predicting emotional states
- Generate preliminary clinical evidence
- Position for larger grants (NIH R01, DARPA, major foundation programs)

**Bottom line (bold):** "Year 1 builds the team and platform. Year 2 proves it works. Each milestone is designed to unlock the next stage of funding."

**Visual:** Horizontal timeline with two blocks. Y1 on the left ("Infrastructure"), Y2 on the right ("Translation"). Key milestones as checkpoints. An arrow extending beyond Y2 suggesting the larger funding trajectory ahead.

**Speaker notes (~90 sec):** Emphasize fiscal discipline: "We're not asking you to fund open-ended research. Each phase has concrete deliverables. And Y2 milestones are specifically designed to qualify us for NIH and major foundation grants — your investment is the catalyst that unlocks 10x more funding."

## Design Principles

- **Visual style:** Minimal, clean, scientific but not clinical. Consistent with the website's aesthetic (dark text on light backgrounds, Inter font family)
- **Color palette:** Align with existing brand — primary near-black (#111), white backgrounds, subtle accent colors
- **No slide should have more than 3 key points** — foundation officers read decks; density kills retention
- **Every slide answers one question** — no slide tries to do two things
- **Speaker notes are essential** — the slides are anchors, not scripts. The presenter carries the narrative
