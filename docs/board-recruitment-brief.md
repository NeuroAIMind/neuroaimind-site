## About NeuroAI Mind Labs

NeuroAI Mind Labs is an independent nonprofit research organization bridging brain science and artificial intelligence to advance mental health.

**Mission:** To uncover how brain-body systems generate emotion and translate these insights into intelligent systems and biomedical technologies that advance mental health.

**Tagline:** Neurons to Mind. Brain to AI.

### What We Do

We study how emotion emerges and evolves across biological scales using a closed-loop computational framework that connects neuroscience and AI. Our approach integrates four layers:

- **Biological Mechanisms** — Neural populations, brain-body coupling
- **Data Integration** — Neural, physiological, and behavioral datasets
- **Computational Modeling** — Predict emotional states, simulate outcomes
- **Actionable Intelligence** — Real-time intervention through closed-loop feedback

This framework produces models that are not only descriptive but actionable — enabling hypothesis testing, guided interventions, and real-time adaptation.

### Stage and Funding

- **Stage:** Pre-revenue, founder-led research nonprofit
- **Current funding target:** $1M-$3M seed from foundations
- **Validation to date:** NSF-funded prior research, publications in NeuroAI and emotion perception, active collaborations with University of Florida faculty

### 24-Month Roadmap

Year 1 builds the research engine. Year 2 proves the model. Each milestone is designed to unlock the next stage of funding.

**Year 1: Build the Engine**

- Recruit core research team (computational neuroscientists, AI/ML researchers, research engineers)
- Build computational infrastructure and data pipelines
- Establish formal research partnerships and data-sharing agreements
- Publish foundational work establishing the closed-loop framework

**Year 2: Prove the Model**

- Launch pilot study applying the framework to a specific condition (e.g., depression, anxiety)
- Develop prototype tool for monitoring/predicting emotional states
- Generate preliminary clinical evidence
- Position for larger grants (NIH R01, major foundation programs)

Year 2 milestones are specifically designed to qualify for NIH and major foundation grants — seed investment is the catalyst that unlocks an order of magnitude more in follow-on funding.

## Why We Are Building a Board

As we pursue seed funding and prepare for our first year of operations, we are assembling a small, working board that will provide governance, strategic guidance, and active support during our most formative stage.

We are seeking **two board members** who bring complementary expertise and are willing to contribute meaningfully.

## Position 1: Scientific Advisor with Funding Expertise

### Role

Provide scientific credibility, validate our research direction, and open doors to funding agencies and foundation program officers.

### Ideal Profile

- Tenured or senior researcher in neuroscience, computational psychiatry, neuroengineering, or a closely related field
- Track record of NIH or NSF-funded research
- Experience serving on foundation scientific advisory boards, grant review panels, or editorial boards
- Familiarity with the intersection of neuroscience and AI/machine learning
- Connections to program officers at relevant foundations (e.g., NIMH, Simons Foundation, Wellcome Trust, BRAIN Initiative affiliates)

### What We Ask

- Attend quarterly board meetings (virtual)
- Review and provide feedback on grant proposals and scientific strategy (estimated 2-4 hours/quarter beyond meetings)
- Make introductions to relevant funders, collaborators, and potential team members
- Lend credibility through board affiliation when engaging with foundations

### What We Offer

- Board membership at an early-stage organization with significant growth trajectory
- Opportunity to shape the scientific direction of a novel research program at the intersection of neuroscience and AI
- Co-authorship opportunities on foundational publications, where appropriate
- A seat at the table as we scale from seed funding to major federal grants
